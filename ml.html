Threat Detective

Pre-trained image classifier deployed on Google Cloud


An image classification website that will tell you if the image you have uploaded is a firearm, knife or a bomb.
Pretrained resnet34 from FastAI used to build classifier by training on custom dataset of three classes.
The dataset was built from scratch using Google Images and using a JavaScript command that downloaded the URLs into a CSV.
FastAI's download functionality was used to create labeled Image Data Bunch which trained the resnet34 for the three classes mentioned.

Good Books Recommender

Predicting Bestsellers and recommending for your genre

Scraped Goodreads.com for reviews and metadata of top 100 NYTimes bestsellers of 2019.

As a team of five, we applied the following analyses:
* What characteristics/themes make a bestseller?
* What sorts of objects/themes on Book covers lead to bestselling books?
* What books can we recommend to user who enters their favorite genres based on two metrics:
Similarity with other users and Sentinment scores of the reviews.

The scoring involved sentiment analysis using Vader and similarity scores using Spacy library.

WIldfire Prediction and Analysis

Predicting causes, location and scale using the power of data

Sourcing data from the US Department of Agriculture, we had wildfire data for the 23 years from 1992-2015.
After initial analyses regarding states, frequency, categories of causes and extracting visualizations, we as a team of five decided to predict the following:
1. Cause of WIldfires given the features that could be known beforehand
2. Probable Wildfire locations and matching with reality
3. Predicting Fire Size, given the geographical and deiscovery features

Next, we analyzed the features importances, to understand better which features are needed to predict the above results accurately and speculated as to the additional features might help.  To know more, have a look at the Medium blog we wrote.

Attack on AI

What if an autonomous car sees a person where there is a Green light?Understanding Object recognition and how they can be attacked adversarially while training, we as a team of six decided to perform this attack one very crucial use case of object recognition and detection - Autonomous vehicles.

We used Faster R-CNN for the Object recognition model, which is trained on the Microsoft COCO dataset.
For the adversarial image we created masks of a walking-man green light symbol and modified the abckground of the image to create perturbations to fool the Neural network.
The perturbations were improved iteratively and by applying gradient descent, till the perturbations seemed unnoticeable to the human eye, but were present significantly enough to fool the algorithm.
